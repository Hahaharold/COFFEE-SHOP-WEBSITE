CAFFEINE MENU

Arabica

Known as one of the most popular and well known types of coffee bean, Arabica beans are the most commonly produced variety and are considered higher quality beans.

Robusta

Robusta is the second most produced in the world. As the name hints, the Robusta bean is both hardy and is also immune to a variety of disease too. Thit type of coffee bean grows best in a hot climate with irregular rainfall and can grow at a number of altitudes too.

Liberica

One of the hardest types of coffee bean to come by is the Liberica bean. they are larger than the other beans and is the only one in the world that is known to have an irregular shape.

Excelsa

Although having recently been classified as a member of the Liberica family, the Excelsa bean is vastly different in taste when compared to the Liberica coffee bean.

Civet

These coffee beans are not directly harvested from trees but from the droppings of Philippine Civet Cat (Paradoxurus Philipinensis) - chooses the ripest and sweetest coffee cherries.

Benguet Arabica

Experts say, Benguet has the best Arabica coffee in the Philippines. It's highland climate is highly suitable for arabica cultivation - the same with Sagada Arabica. Their produce is for both local consumption and international export to Spain.

COFFEE MENU

Espresso

The espresso, also known as a short black, is approximately 1 oz. of highly concentrated coffee. Although simple in appearance, it can be difficult to master.

Americano

Americanos are popular breakfast drinks and thought to have originated during World War II. Soldiers would add water to their coffee to extend their rations farther. The water dilutes the espresso while still maintaining a high level of caffeine.

Red Eye

The red eye's purpose is to add a boost of caffeine to your standard cup of coffee.

Cappuccino

This creamy coffee drink is usually consumed at breakfast time in Italy and is loved in the United States as well. It is usually associated with indulgence and comfort because of its thick foam layer and additional flavorings that can be added to it.

Black Eye

The black eye is just the doubled version of the red eye and is very high in caffeine.

Cortado

The cortado takes the macchiato one step further by evenly balancing the espresso with warm milk in order to reduce the acidity.
